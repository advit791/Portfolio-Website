// Why CAD first?
Everything starts in Blender.

Before anything gets printed, every part lives in a Blender scene. I model to actual servo dimensions, account for print tolerances, test joint clearances, and verify everything fits. QuadBot has 121+ objects across its collection hierarchy — body panels, leg segments, servo horns, brackets, and wire routing channels.

Catching a clearance problem in 3D costs nothing. Printing a part that doesn't fit costs hours of time and filament. CAD first, always.

// Always Learning

What I'm Into

🦿
Biomechanics
Studying how insects and animals walk to design QuadBot's gait. Every leg phase, timing, and joint angle matters for smooth locomotion.
🧠
Edge AI
Running AI inference on microcontrollers. The assistant project pushes me to optimize LLM calls and pipe responses to hardware in real time.
⚡
Power Systems
Managing multiple servos drawing current simultaneously. I've learned about voltage regulators, caps, and why robots randomly reset (it's always the power supply).
🖨️
Print Tolerances
FDM has quirks. I design 0.2mm clearance into press-fit joints, orient parts for layer strength, and pick the right infill for structural vs cosmetic pieces.
📡
Sensor Fusion
Combining IMU, ultrasonic, and encoder data for better spatial awareness. Next step for QuadBot: self-correcting balance using feedback loops.
🔧
Iteration
Every project has at least 3 physical prototypes. The arm went through 5 gripper versions. Failure is data — I document what broke and why, then fix it.
