QuadBot

A four-legged walking robot fully designed from scratch in Blender 5.0. The chassis, servo mounts, and leg mechanisms were all CAD'd and 3D printed. Powered by multiple servo motors, an Arduino, and a 9V LiION battery — with LED strip lighting because why not. Currently in active development.

Robot Arm

AI Assistant

A physical AI assistant device housed in a custom 3D-printed enclosure with an LCD display, speaker, and status LEDs. Integrates with an LLM API to answer questions, show responses on screen, and provide audio feedback — a desktop companion with brains.

Weather Checker

Design Process

From CAD to Reality

Blender 5.0

Everything starts as geometry.

Before a single wire is connected or a servo installed, every part lives in Blender. I model the full assembly — body, legs, brackets, mounts — and test fit and clearance in 3D before committing to a print.

QuadBot alone has 121+ objects in its scene collection, each modeled to fit real-world servo dimensions and print tolerances.

Real Hardware

Then it gets messy.

Once the parts are printed, the real work begins — routing servo cables, connecting driver boards, soldering LEDs, and getting the firmware to talk to everything at once.

The wire chaos is a feature. It means it's real.
